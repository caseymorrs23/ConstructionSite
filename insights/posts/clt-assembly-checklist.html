CLT Assembly Checklist for Custom Homes

Published September 24, 2025 • GQC Insights

On every job, predictability beats heroics. At GQC, commercial and residential (CLT) framing runs on a simple model: align the plan, stage the work, protect the building, and measure what matters. This approach lowers risk for general contractors while keeping subs coordinated and on pace.

What GCs want: a framing partner that opens levels on schedule and returns floors dry. That’s why our crews combine production layout with envelope checkpoints—plumb, line, square; window and door opening verification; WRB tie‑ins; and safe crane rhythm. We keep supers informed on look‑ahead, laydown, and crane windows so other trades plan around clear dates.

Quality without churn: we bake QC into the way we frame. Blocking and backing follow hardware schedules. Flashing, sealants, and tie‑ins are verified before close‑in. This prevents punchlist churn and warranty callbacks later.

Close the loop fast: production boards and simple metrics tell us where to help, not who to blame. We reinforce wins and fix friction early—why GCs bring us back for the next building.
