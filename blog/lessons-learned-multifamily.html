Lessons Learned from Large Multi‑Family Projects in Washington

Insights for GCs pricing large, schedule-driven work.

What We See Most Often

Washington’s weather, topography, and permitting create unique constraints. We see the best outcomes where envelope decisions are locked early and site logistics are modeled before mobilization. The worst schedules come from late openings coordination and incomplete shop drawings.

Our lessons learned library informs every new job—details, durations, and manpower that have worked on buildings of similar scale.

Owner Priorities and Turnover

Owners want predictable turnover and clean common areas. We protect both by emphasizing dry‑in pace and stair/amenity framing. Delivering these spaces early lets other trades gain float without impacting the critical path.
